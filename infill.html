Understanding 3D Print Infill

Infill is the internal structure of a 3D print. Higher infill percentages make parts stronger and heavier, but with diminishing returns above 40–50%.

The infill pattern matters as much as the percentage. Gyroid and cubic patterns distribute stress in all directions (isotropic). Grid and lines are anisotropic — strong in some directions, weak in others. For most functional parts, 20–40% gyroid or cubic infill with 3–4 walls provides the best balance.
